About Dave

A Compassionate Guide on Your Journey

Hi, I'm Dave Dyck — a counsellor, listener, and your partner in rediscovering who you are beneath life's pressures and pain. I hold a Master of Counselling (MC) and am a Canadian Certified Counsellor (CCC), registered with the Canadian Counselling and Psychotherapy Association (CCPA).

You don't have to figure everything out on your own. If you're here, there's a good chance something feels off. Maybe you're overwhelmed, stuck in your thoughts, or unsure what direction to take next. Whatever brought you here — you don't have to carry it alone.

I offer counselling that is grounded, practical, and focused on helping you feel more in control of your thoughts and emotions. This isn't about overanalyzing everything or giving you generic advice — it's about helping you understand what's going on beneath the surface and giving you tools you can actually use in your day-to-day life.

Together, we work to:

  • Reduce overwhelm and anxiety
  • Understand patterns that keep you stuck
  • Build clarity and confidence in your decisions
  • Move toward a direction that feels right for you

Sessions are a space where you can speak openly without judgment. You don't need to show up with everything figured out. We go at a pace that works for you — whether you need structure, space to process, or practical strategies to move forward.

You might be navigating:

  • Anxiety and overthinking
  • Feeling stuck or directionless
  • Trauma or past experiences
  • Men's mental health challenges
  • Motor vehicle accident recovery (ICBC counselling)
  • Concussion-related emotional impacts

Taking the first step can feel like the hardest part. You don't need to commit to anything right away — a simple conversation is a good place to start.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is everything I share confidential?
+
Absolutely. Everything discussed in our sessions is strictly confidential. The only exceptions are situations involving imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, as required by law — and I'll always explain these limits clearly before we begin.
How do I know if counselling is right for me?
+
If you're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or simply want a space to think things through with support — counselling can help. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit. A free 20-minute consultation is a great no-pressure way to find out if we're a good fit.
Do you offer virtual sessions?
+
Yes! I offer secure, confidential video sessions for clients anywhere in BC. Virtual sessions are just as effective as in-person and give you the comfort of being in your own space. All you need is a private spot and a stable internet connection.
How many sessions will I need?
+
This varies for everyone. Some people find clarity in just a few sessions; others prefer ongoing monthly support. We'll check in regularly and you always decide the pace. There's no minimum commitment — just show up when it feels right.
Is counselling covered by insurance?
+
Yes — and I make it as easy as possible. I am a registered ICBC service provider and can direct bill for motor vehicle accident (MVA) claims, including concussion support. I also offer direct billing to many extended health insurance providers, so there's often no upfront cost. Additionally, I accept autism funding as an approved service provider. Contact me before your first session and I'll confirm your coverage and handle the paperwork.
What if I need to cancel or reschedule?
+
Life happens — I get it. I ask for at least 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations (under 24 hours) may be subject to a partial fee. Just reach out and we'll find a time that works for you.
