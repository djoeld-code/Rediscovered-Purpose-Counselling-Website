Why Driving Feels Scary
After a Car Accident

📅 May 2026 ⏱ 5 min read Dave Dyck, MC, CCC Nanaimo, BC

Driving anxiety after an accident is one of the most common trauma responses. Here is why it happens and what actually helps.

Book a Free Consultation →

If getting back behind the wheel after an accident feels terrifying — even for short trips, even months later — you are not being irrational. Your nervous system is doing exactly what it was designed to do.

Your Nervous System Learned Something

When you are in a car accident, your brain processes it as a life threat. Your amygdala — the brain's threat-detection centre — recorded the experience as dangerous. Now anything associated with that experience can trigger the same alarm: getting in a car, a similar intersection, a sudden noise in traffic.

Fear of driving is one of the most common responses following a motor vehicle accident. Your nervous system experienced something threatening, and it's doing its job by trying to keep you safe. If this is lasting more than a few weeks, counselling after a car accident can help you work through it — and in BC, it may be covered by ICBC at no cost to you.

Common Signs of Post-Accident Driving Anxiety

  • Heart racing or chest tightening when you get in a car
  • Constant scanning for danger while driving — hypervigilance
  • Panic attacks while driving or thinking about driving
  • Avoiding certain roads, intersections, or times of day
  • Needing someone with you to feel safe in the car
  • Intrusive images of the accident while driving

Why It Gets Worse Without Help

Avoidance provides short-term relief — but it teaches your nervous system that driving is indeed dangerous. The more you avoid it, the stronger the fear becomes.

What you're experiencing may be connected to trauma — your brain has learned to associate driving with danger. Working with a counsellor who offers trauma counselling can help reduce the emotional charge that your nervous system has attached to driving, so that everyday situations stop triggering that alarm response.

What Actually Helps

Effective treatment addresses both the trauma and the nervous system response — trauma-focused therapy to reduce the emotional charge of accident memories, nervous system regulation tools, and structured steps back toward driving confidence. If your accident happened in BC, ICBC counselling may cover this at no cost.

Hypervigilance, intrusive thoughts, and avoidance are all signs your nervous system is still on high alert. If any of this sounds familiar, you may also find it helpful to read more about hypervigilance after an accident — and what can be done about it.

How long does driving anxiety last after an accident?
Without treatment it can persist for months or years. With trauma-focused counselling, many people see meaningful improvement within 6–10 sessions.
Can ICBC cover treatment for driving anxiety?
Yes — as a registered ICBC provider I can direct bill ICBC for driving anxiety treatment after an MVA.
Is driving anxiety a sign of PTSD?
It can be. Driving anxiety is often a PTSD symptom. See Trauma Counselling Nanaimo for more.

You Do Not Have to Avoid Driving Forever
