I'm Dave Dyck, a Canadian Certified Counsellor (CCC) based in Nanaimo. I'm a registered ICBC provider and I offer virtual counselling sessions to Courtenay residents with direct billing to ICBC — meaning in most cases there's no cost to you.

What You're Entitled To After an Accident

  • 12 pre-approved counselling sessions within the first 12 weeks
  • No doctor's referral needed if you start within that window
  • Coverage applies regardless of who was at fault
  • Virtual sessions are fully covered — join from home
  • Extensions available if your recovery requires more support
  • Family members may be covered too — see ICBC Family Member Coverage

The Counselling Gap on Vancouver Island

The Courtenay-Comox area is growing fast, but mental health resources haven't kept pace — particularly for specialized MVA trauma support. Driving anxiety, PTSD, sleep disruption, emotional shutdown — these don't go away on their own, and they deserve proper support.

Virtual counselling means you get the same quality of care as someone in a city, without leaving your home and without a wait.

What I Help With

  • PTSD and trauma from the accident
  • Driving anxiety and fear of being in vehicles
  • Anxiety, panic attacks, and hypervigilance
  • Sleep disruption and emotional exhaustion
  • Depression and withdrawal
  • Concussion-related mental health impacts

How to Get Started

  1. Contact me with your ICBC claim number
  2. I confirm your coverage and book your first session
  3. I direct bill ICBC — you focus on recovering
